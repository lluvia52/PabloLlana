Bio

Pablo Llana is from Tijuana, Baja California Mexico. His start in the arts goes back to   1997, when he began attending plastic arts courses at Casa de la Cultura de Tijuana (Tijuana’s home of the arts).

In 1999 he decides to be a full time profesional visual artist and in this manner be able to satisfy his calling and express, in an ironic fashion that which is evident and incongrous in everyday life; it is also in 1999 when he gets honorable mention in the XII Plastic Baja California Biennial Exposition.

From the year 2000 to 2002 he estudies in the program OS Artistic Profesionalism for Visual Artists, (Propavis) sponsored by Conaculta at Baja California’s Center for Human Studies, under renowned profesional artists and masters.

During those years, he participated in more than 20 local and international collective exhibitions such as “Tapetes 01” at Tijuana’s Cultural Center (CECUT), and “Pintura Fresca” (Fresh Painting) at the Luckman fine arts gallery Of the California State  University of Los Angeles, for which has was mentioned by the Los Angeles Times and LA Weekly. By the same time he presents succesful individual exhibitions, like “Entre el Riesgo y el Castigo” at the galeria de la ciudad, in Tijuana Baja  California; also in Tijuana, “Makiyate Makiyaje” (Makeup--Make-­up) at the Instituto de Cultura de Baja California.

In the year 2005 he participated in the International Collective Exhibition at Foro de Cultura y las Artes in Madrid, Spain.

From 2005 to 2009 the artist goes through an introspective period, during which the arts  reappear as something natural, not compromised nor forced upon him, his reative   production increases, and, from the year 2010 his artistic quality takes a new meaning as shown in his work “Tijuana Makes Me Happy” ( in which his themewas “The  joker” as he was played by Heath Ledger in the movie Batman.) in a public intervention in revolucion ave, and “Obra Negra, an approach to Tijuana’s visual culture development”, 2011 at el Cubo, in Tijuana’s Cultural Center (CECUT).

In 2011 he earns honorable mentions when he takes part in two biennial events: The  13th Northwest Visual Arts and the 5th Miradas National Exhibition. 

Currently he is in the process of developing a body of bi and three-­‐dimensional work based upon two concepts: capitalism-­‐consumerism  and  obesity-­‐identity, using trash food wrappings as his color palette; families from San Diego and Los Angeles California as well from Guadalajara, Mexicali and Tijuana contribute to his work providing the wrappings thanks to a network of enthusiastic supporters. This innovative pictorial technique of his own creation gives new form and meaning to what is regarded by most people as just material refuse.

Thanks to this technique he was selected to participate in the 6th International Aire Arte Contemporary Textil Exhibition at the Oaxaca recycling textile art salon, 2011 selection, also being invited to present an individual exhibition at the 9th TC3 Cubo Textil Contemporaneo “Oaxaca Desde Aca”, at the Oaxaca Textile Museum, 2011.

In 2013 there is a turning point in Pablo Llana’s career, when he produces the “Mouthful” series (his 4th individual show) exhibited with great success at Oaxaca’s Graphic Arts Institute (IAGO) in february. It is worth mentioning that Pablo is the first artist from Tijuanato have exhibited his work in that reconognized Institution Chaired by master Francisco Toledo.

I this past april he exhibits “Yo Codigo Comunidad” (I Community Code) in CECUT, Tijuana’s Cultural Center; it is a series of CQ codes which can be deciphered by the public through electronic devices, finding messages, sayings, statistics and other types of information regarding the obesity problem. It is also in april when he was invited to take part in “Trono De Los De A Pie”, a collective exhibition  in the company of two hundred invited artists, at la Calera, Oaxaca  Mexico.

You may find a journalistc reportin the anniversary issue of “Arte al Dia Mexico” (march-­‐ april 2013) on the “Mouthful” series, in the guest artist section.
